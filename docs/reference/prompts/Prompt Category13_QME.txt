You are an assistant that summarizes QME (Qualified Medical Evaluator) and AME (Agreed Medical Evaluator) reports.

** You will Present the summary in the form of a paragraph and not bullet points.
** Summarize directly without discussion or introduction sentences.
** Some documents might not have all the points. If a document does not have a specific point, do not include it.
** VERY IMPORTANT: DO NOT create points on your own since these are medical documents.
** It is acceptable if the summary is a bit long.
** Be as accurate as possible as if my life depends on it.

IMPORTANT: If the text contains an MRR (Medical Record Review) in it, do not take info from the MRR rather for the points, rather base your summary on the text outside of the MRR. Treat the MRR in the document as if it does not exist.

# Diagnoses (Diagnostic Impression)
** It is VERY IMPORTANT to include the diagnostic for the current case rather using information from the MRR included in the document.
** Make the diagnoses numbered.
** Include all the diagnoses in detail. Be very specific and do not combine points. Be direct. My life depends on it.
** Try to use the same wording for the diagnoses and write them in full without summarizing them.

# Impairment Rating
** DO NOT confuse Work Status with Impairment rating.
** For the Impairment Rating, we want the Final AMA Whole Person Impairment with the exact language of the impairment rating for each body part. The subdivision and the total impairment rating is very important.

# Causation
** Include the causation in the output
** Be as detailed as you can.

Employer: California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation Parole and Community Services. Occupation: Correctional Officer from 2006 to 2012; sergeant from 2012 to 2016; Parole Agent for the last 2 years. Occupational History: Began working for employer in September of 2006. Works 41 hours a week. Duties include helping convicted felons attempt to reintegrate into society; assist with resources that state offers ex-felons; provide public safety; handled a case load of 55 parolees/felons; perform arrest reports; perform surveillance, search and seizure; made court appearances, arrest people; wrote reports; driving 2,500 miles a month; drove all North County San Diego as well as down the coast, Vista, Oceanside and Down Town; handled male and female adults; spends 90% of his time at a desk; made home contacts; community contacts, home, business, in field in general; wears protective gear, vest and bullet proof vest, gun, flash light/Sam Browne belt. Sets his own daily hours. Physical requirements included standing, walking, bending, stooping, climbing, kneeling, crawling, twisting and turning at waist level, reaching at or above shoulder level, pushing, pulling, gripping, grasping, other gross upper extremity movements, and fine hand manipulation. Required to lift and carry items weighing more than 20 pounds. Required to perform arrests, 1 to 3 times per week.
Otherwise, he spends 100% of the work day either driving or typing, using both hands equally. HPI: Patient stated that on 02/14/17 [as per report], he was driving his vehicle to work traveling on the middle lane of the freeway going approximately 55 to 60 miles per hour. Rain was pouring when a vehicle to the right of him moved into his lane while making hard stop on their brakes. He was forced to do a hard stop; lost control of his vehicle and it hydroplaned. It went over 3 lanes, crashed into the center divider, then went to the right and went down an embankment. He did not lose consciousness and after he gathered his senses, he called 911. Stayed in his vehicle until the fire department showed up. He was taken out of his vehicle and states that his adrenaline was high, and did not feel any, specific pain but was disoriented. Stabilized and transported to Sharp Memorial Hospital where he was examined in the trauma department. Underwent multiple x-rays and CT scans, and no fractures were noted. Once he calmed down, he recalls experiencing low back pain and locking up of his low back. He was taken off work and stayed home to rest. His employer referred him to U.S. HealthWorks where he was examined. X-rays of the low back were performed. He was referred to acupuncture and physical therapy. Two months into his injury, he requested treatment for the left shoulder due to persistent aching pain and restricted ROM. An MRI study of the left shoulder was ordered, which demonstrated a labrum tear. He was referred to orthopedist, Dr. William Holland, whom he felt minimized the findings and his complaints. Left subacromial injection was performed without relief. Requested a second opinion and was referred to Dr. Steven Kelley. He was maintaining his left arm adducted as he guarded from the time of injury until surgery, or 4 months. Feels this contributed to substantial weakness and tightness of the left pecs, which impeded his recovery. In July of 2017, he underwent left shoulder surgery which was performed by Dr. Kelley. Referred to postoperative physical therapy sometime in late September of 2017. He was immobilized when not resting, for much of that prospective period until therapy enabled him to discontinue use. He was prescribed Norco and on 10/19/17, he was administered a cortisone injection but is unsure where in the shoulder. He was feeling worse pain and continued experiencing restricted ROM. Participated in therapy through March or April of 2018. Estimates he had 20 therapy visits. Highly compliant with an independent gym program using machines in a limited range. Avoids free weights. No acupuncture. Self-procures chiropractic treatment regularly. Returned to regular work duties on 03/01/18. Tolerates this well, though about once a week, he requires use of ibuprofen to control pain. Pain in the left shoulder can range from 2/10 to 10/10. It was consistently 10/10 preop, so is generally satisfied with his outcome. Present Complaints: Complained of 5/10 left shoulder pain, aggravated by activities. Experiences pinching sensation, the nerve inflames in forearm, left trap/deltoid to left forearm. Pain radiates from the trap through deltoid to the forearm and ring and pinky finger. Limited ROM.
Instability. Unable to sleep on the left shoulder and is awaken from the pain. Notes atrophy right above deltoid and trap connect. Prior to injury, he was lifting 80 pounds. He can now lift about 35 to 50 pounds using both hands, 80 pounds with the right. Regarding push/pull, he was capable of 70 pounds or more prior to injury, now limited to 35 pounds. He has left volar elbow/proximal forearm discomfort. Also complained of continuous dull aching pain in the neck and trapezius area. Pain increases by the end of his work day. Applying ice and heat to his neck and stretching help the neck pain. Motrin 800 mg helps alleviate the pain. Notes pain to the triceps and volar elbow, with episodic numbness, and to a lesser degree, fourth digits. He has difficulty with sustained left grasp. Further complained of 2-3/10 low back pain. Low back tightens throughout the day. He had an increase of pre-existing migraines after the injury, but states that this condition is back to baseline now. Takes migraine medications as needed. He has gained 20 pounds as a result of inactivity since injury. Unable to reengage in aerobic conditioning either due to pain. Sleep is generally broken, and at most he gets 3 to 4 hours of uninterrupted sleep due to left shoulder pain. He can no longer play golf, and his gym routine is much more limited. PMH: Previously suffered from high blood pressure and was prescribed medication. He first took medication in 2011/2012. Takes aspirin 81 mg 3 times a week. PSH: Left shoulder arthroscopic labral repair on 07/26/17. Right shoulder surgery in 2011. Previous Injury: In 1998/1999, while working with an unrecalled plumbing company, he suffered right wrist injury/tear and subsequently underwent right wrist cartilage repair. He was represented and the claims were settled. PE: Elevated blood pressure 153/102. Weight 216 pounds. Cervical spine: Tenderness in left greater than right upper trapezius, left rhomboids and left T1-T6 paraspinal muscles. Foraminal compression (Spurling's) test is positive on the left shoulder. Limited ROM in all planes. Atrophy on left posterior and lateral deltoid. Left upper extremities: Strength: external rotation 4+/5. Left shoulder: Tenderness on the pectoralis insertion, subacromial and subdeltoid. Impingement test is positive. Status post arthroscopy, healed. Difficult to test internal and external rotations due to reduced abduction. Bilateral shoulder ROM limited in all planes. Deep pain at volar left elbow, medial to brachioradialis. Thoracolumbar spine and lower extremities: Pain to palpation about the left T1-T6. Limited ROM in all planes. Final Diagnostic Impression: 1) Status post left shoulder arthroscopic labral repair by Dr Steven Kelley, 07/26/17. 2) Residual postoperative left shoulder weakness, deltoid atrophy, loss of ROM, ADL limitation and pain. 3) Cervical strain with possible left radiculitis. 4) Thoracic and left shoulder myofascial pain syndrome. 5) Reported 20-pound weight gain due to inactivity and aerobic deconditioning. Impairment Rating (AMA Guides 5th Edition): Cervical spine DRE Category Il due to persistent non-verifiable radicular symptoms and loss of ROM, with 5% WI. For the left shoulder, based on the ROM Method, total upper extremity impairment of 20% converts to 12% WPI. Pain-related impairment/effects of treatment of 3% WI. Using the Combined Values Chart, final Whole Person Impairment of 19%. Work Limitations: Capable of performing his usual and customary duties without restrictions. Patient reports being on TTD for a year post-injury, until returning full duties on 03/01/18, 41 hours per week. This period of TTD was reasonable. Causation/ Apportionment: Patient's need for medical care stems from the industrial injury of 02/27/17. 100% of the permanent disability/whole person impairment in this case is due to the industrial injury of 02/27/17. 0% is due to the industrially-related injuries of 07/06/99, 11/26/09, CT 05/01/10-04/19/11. Prior industrial injuries do not overlap with the 02/27/17 industrial injury. Future Medical Care: In relation to the cervical spine, thoracic spine, lumbar spine and left shoulder conditions, patient should be awarded future medical care for flare-ups that would be reasonably expected for these conditions. Expect additional conservative therapy treatments over a year's period, to include acupuncture, physical therapy and chiropractic care. May require pharmaceutical agents to include, but not limited to, analgesics and NSAIDs. May require additional diagnostic studies (including CT/MRI scans and EMG/NCV studies) to monitor the potential progression of industrial injury. Access to future orthopedic consultations and cortisone injections should his condition deteriorate. No further diagnostics at this time as he reports general satisfaction with his postop recovery. However, his PTP should consider MRI of the cervical spine, electrodiagnostic testing to include EMG of the atrophied deltoid heads, and MRI of the left shoulder. Consideration should also be given to a round of 8 physical therapy sessions to focus on aerobic conditioning for weight loss, and to assure his current gym routine is safe and effective. As much of his pain is myofascial, the claimant may benefit from anesthetic trigger point injections. These ideally would be performed in association with physical therapy or chiropractic, so that the injected muscles can then be rehabilitated.
